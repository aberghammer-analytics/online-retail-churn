Who's about to stop buying, and how do we know?

This report walks through the full pipeline — from 824k raw transaction line items down to a per-customer model that ranks who is most likely to churn. It is built for two readers at once: the business story sits up top, and every technical detail lives one click away inside the ▸ technical panels. Everything below is live — drag the sliders, sort the tables, explore the data.

Churn is defined as no product purchase in the final 90 days of the data. All features are measured strictly before that cutoff so the label can never leak into the inputs.

Technical detail — preprocessing & validation

Right-skewed spend/cadence features () get a log1p transform before standard scaling, so the linear model isn't dominated by a few whales. Tree models are scale-invariant, so the forest just median-imputes. Validation is 5-fold StratifiedKFold on the training split; the forest is tuned with RandomizedSearchCV (10 draws). The test set is touched exactly once, at the end.

Leakage guard: the train/test split is stratified on the label, and every feature is computed strictly before the 90-day cutoff — the same cutoff that defines churn — so future information never enters the inputs.

04

Pick a threshold

The model outputs a probability, not a yes/no. Choosing the cut-off is a business call: a low threshold catches more churners (high recall) but wastes outreach on the loyal; a high threshold is precise but misses people. Drag to feel the trade-off on the random forest's test-set predictions.
